About Me

Documenting Life. Reimagining It in Print.

I am Mugema Brian, a Ugandan visual artist and graduate of the Margaret Trowell School of Industrial and Fine Art at Makerere University, where I earned a Bachelor of Fine Art. I also served as the Guild Representative Council for the school — a role that deepened my connection to the creative community I grew up in.

My practice lives at the intersection of photography, videography, and printmaking. I am drawn to the quiet, unnoticed moments of everyday life — the gestures, textures, and environments that carry deep emotional and cultural meaning. What I capture through the camera becomes the foundation for bold, graphic print compositions that transform lived experience into expressive visual form.

What I Do

Practice & Disciplines

Exploring the world through the lens, the frame, and the press — three disciplines, one visual language.

Documentary Photography

Capturing the subtle moments, gestures and environments of everyday life that carry rich emotional and cultural meaning. Composition, light and texture are at the heart of every frame.

Videography & Film

Recording human presence, movement and social atmosphere through the moving image. Video as a tool for studying form, narrative and lived experience.

Printmaking

Translating photographic moments into bold, graphic print compositions. Strong lines, contrast and expressive mark-making transform images into powerful visual statements.

Visual Storytelling

Weaving together photography, video and print into cohesive narratives that document and reimagine identity, environment and contemporary Ugandan life.

Composition & Form Studies

A deep focus on the formal elements of visual art — line, shape, texture, contrast and form — as the foundation for all creative and conceptual work.

Community & Cultural Practice

Shaped by the vibrant cultural energy of Kampala and the communities Brian engages with. Art as a reflection of place, people and lived experience.
