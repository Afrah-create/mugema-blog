About Me

Documenting Life. Reimagining It in Print.

I'm Mugema Brian, a Ugandan visual artist currently studying at the Margaret Trowell School of Fine Art at Makerere University. Beyond my studio work, I serve as the Guild Representative Council, a responsibility that keeps me connected to the creative community here in Kampala.

I work across photography, videography, and printmaking. I'm drawn to quiet, unnoticed moments in everyday life—the gestures and textures that tell stories about who we are. My camera captures these moments, and then I translate them into bold, graphic prints. It's how I process the world around me.

What I Do

Practice & Disciplines

Exploring the world through the lens, the frame, and the press — three disciplines, one visual language.

Documentary Photography

Capturing the subtle moments, gestures and environments of everyday life that carry rich emotional and cultural meaning. Composition, light and texture are at the heart of every frame.

Videography & Film

Recording human presence, movement and social atmosphere through the moving image. Video as a tool for studying form, narrative and lived experience.

Printmaking

Translating photographic moments into bold, graphic print compositions. Strong lines, contrast and expressive mark-making transform images into powerful visual statements.
